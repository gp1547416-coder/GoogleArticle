How to Understand the Evolution of Google

Co-authored by Tech Expert | Last Updated: March 18, 2026

Google is no longer just a search engine; it is a global ecosystem. Whether you are curious about its humble beginnings in a garage or its current status as a $2.5 trillion AI leader, understanding Google requires looking at how it organized the world's information.

1

Trace the Origins from "Backrub" to Google.

[Image: Larry Page and Sergey Brin at Stanford]

Born in the research halls of Stanford University in 1996, Google began as a project by Larry Page and Sergey Brin. Originally titled "Backrub" due to its ability to analyze "backlinks" to estimate the importance of a site, the search engine utilized a revolutionary algorithm called **PageRank**. This wasn't just a list of words; it was a map of human trust across the web. By 1998, with a $100,000 check from Andy Bechtolsheim, Google Inc. was officially incorporated in Susan Wojcicki’s garage. This culture of innovation led to the 2004 IPO and the subsequent acquisition of YouTube in 2006, which solidified Google as a media titan. Today, as a subsidiary of Alphabet Inc., Google dominates search with over 8.5 billion daily queries and continues to push boundaries into artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and quantum systems, maintaining its original mission to make information universally accessible and useful.

2

Learn to navigate the Alphabet Era.

In 2015, Google underwent a massive restructuring, creating a parent company called Alphabet. This allowed the "core" Google (Search, Maps, YouTube, Android) to thrive while "Other Bets" like Waymo (self-driving cars) and Verily (life sciences) could innovate independently.
